Panel Discussions

Morning Panel Technological and Data Challenges

This panel will focus on identifying issues relating to our abilities to manage, model, store, transmit, and validate information on financial market operations. The panel would consider the types of socio-technical issues discussed in the first panel. However, it would focus more sharply on how technological and data changes in the field affect supervision and regulation.

Panel Moderator: John Malitzis Executive Vice President, Market Regulation, Financial Industry Regulatory Authority (FINRA)

- PanelistsSandra Cannon : Deputy Chief Data Officer, Federal Reserve Board

- Robert Colby : Chief Legal Officer, FINRA

- Louiqa Raschid : Professor, University of Maryland

- David Saul : Senior Vice President & Chief Scientist, State Street

Afternoon Panel Organizational factors

This panel will focus on how organizational factors affect information sharing and coordination in related business processes, risk management, and internal and external supervision and control. In a recent paper, Mark D. Flood and William Nichols of the Office of Financial Research, and Allan Mendelowitz, Strategic Advisor of Deloitte Consulting, commented that “Even in processes involving a large degree of automation, a ‘human in the loop’ will typically be a binding constraint” (“Monitoring Financial Stability in a Complex World,” 2012, p. 23).
